4. Description of Service

HomeChata is a comprehensive neighborhood intelligence platform that empowers home buyers, renters, and individuals to make informed decisions about where they live. By synthesizing data across public safety, environmental quality, demographic context, natural hazard exposure, and community amenities into a single, on-demand report for any address in the United States, HomeChata delivers high-value neighborhood intelligence — aggregated, processed, and presented on a single platform — giving users instant access to insights that would otherwise demand extensive research across fragmented, hard-to-navigate public sources. Behind every report is a sophisticated data infrastructure purpose-built to serve people making one of the most important decisions of their lives.

HomeChata is an informational intelligence tool and is not a real estate brokerage, investment advisor, legal advisor, consumer reporting agency, or background check service. Nothing within the App constitutes real estate, financial, legal, or safety advice.

What HomeChata Delivers

A HomeChata neighborhood report provides a comprehensive intelligence snapshot across multiple dimensions, including:

  • Crime and public safety data, including historical trends and incident breakdowns
  • Registered sex offender proximity within defined radius ranges
  • Recent local crime news and public safety incidents
  • Current environmental quality indicators, including air quality conditions
  • Demographic and economic context for the surrounding census area, drawn from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey
  • Race and ethnicity composition for the surrounding census area
  • Flood hazard zone classification from FEMA's National Flood Hazard Layer
  • Federal disaster declaration history from FEMA records
  • Natural hazard risk ratings from FEMA's National Risk Index
  • Proximity and access to essential amenities — schools, hospitals, urgent care, daycare, and grocery stores
  • Lifestyle amenities — parks, gyms, restaurants, transit, and more
  • Nearby cities and regional community context
  • Downloadable PDF report for offline reference and sharing

Nature of Service

HomeChata presents verified, factual data compiled from authoritative sources. The App does not generate opinions, assign scores, issue ratings, or make recommendations regarding any neighborhood, address, or community. Users are solely responsible for drawing their own conclusions from the data presented and for any decisions made in connection therewith.

6. Data Accuracy and Limitations

General Disclaimer

HomeChata aggregates, processes, and presents neighborhood intelligence data from a broad spectrum of government, institutional, and licensed sources. While we are committed to delivering high-quality, meaningful data insights, the nature of large-scale public data means that no dataset is without limitation. HomeChata does not warrant the absolute accuracy, completeness, or real-time currency of any data presented within the App. Data is refreshed periodically and may not reflect the most recent conditions at any given address. In circumstances where comprehensive data is unavailable for a specific location, contextual estimates or regional data may be presented to ensure a complete and useful experience.

Crime and Public Safety Data

HomeChata presents crime and public safety data sourced from government law enforcement databases and institutional reporting programs. This data is subject to reporting cycles and may reflect conditions from prior periods rather than real-time activity. Coverage and reporting standards vary across jurisdictions, and not all law enforcement agencies participate in national reporting programs, which may result in data gaps in certain areas.

Crime statistics reflect reported offenses only and do not capture unreported incidents. HomeChata presents this data as a factual reference tool only. We do not evaluate, score, or render judgments on neighborhood safety. This information should not be relied upon as the sole basis for any decision related to safety, housing, or relocation.

HomeChata's process for matching an address to its responsible law enforcement agency is based on city and county data, and may not correctly identify the actual reporting agency for certain address types — including those served by tribal, federal, university, or state police jurisdictions, or those in unincorporated or special-district areas. For such addresses, HomeChata may display data for the surrounding city or county rather than the agency actually patrolling the location. Users should verify that the reporting agency named in the report matches the agency they expect to serve that address.

Sex Offender Data

HomeChata delivers registered sex offender proximity intelligence as part of its comprehensive public safety awareness offering, drawing directly from official government sex offender registry programs administered at the state, territorial, and federal levels. Registry data is maintained and updated by individual jurisdictions in accordance with their respective statutory requirements and reporting cycles. As registry standards, geographic coverage, and update frequencies vary across jurisdictions, HomeChata presents this information as a current-as-available reference, which may not reflect the absolute real-time status of any individual registration at the moment of a given search.

This information is provided as a public awareness resource within a broader neighborhood intelligence context. It must not be used to harass, threaten, discriminate against, or take any adverse action against any individual. HomeChata is not a consumer reporting agency as defined under the Fair Credit Reporting Act and does not provide background screening, tenant screening, or employment screening services.

Demographics and Census Data

HomeChata presents demographic, economic, and racial/ethnic composition data sourced from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey (ACS) 5-Year Estimates. ACS data is derived from a rolling statistical sample of households and carries inherent margins of error. HomeChata does not modify, recompute, or derive Census Bureau values; figures shown reflect the source agency's published estimates and are subject to revision in subsequent ACS releases.

Census data is published at the Census Tract level — a geographic area containing approximately 1,200 to 8,000 residents — not at the level of any individual address or property. The figures presented describe the broader census tract surrounding the searched address and may not reflect the specific block, building, or unit. Neighborhoods within a single tract can vary significantly; values shown are area-level statistics, not predictions or representations about any particular property or its residents.

To convert a street address into a Census Tract, HomeChata uses the U.S. Census Bureau's official geocoding service. This service may fail to resolve, or may resolve to a nearby location rather than the exact address entered, for certain address types — including tribal lands, military bases, federal facilities, newly built developments, and some rural addresses. Users should verify that the tract and place names shown in their report correspond to the location they intended. HomeChata does not modify, estimate, or correct the geocoding results returned by the Census Bureau.

Use of demographic data — including race, ethnicity, national origin, family composition, or income — to make, influence, or facilitate any housing decision in violation of the Fair Housing Act or any applicable state or local fair housing law is strictly prohibited. See Section 9 (Fair Housing Compliance) for additional terms governing the use of HomeChata data.

Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) Data

HomeChata presents three categories of data sourced from the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA): flood hazard zone classifications drawn from FEMA's National Flood Hazard Layer (NFHL), federal disaster declaration history drawn from FEMA's OpenFEMA Disaster Declarations Summaries, and natural hazard risk ratings drawn from FEMA's National Risk Index (NRI). All three datasets are made publicly available by FEMA and are presented within the App as published by the source agency. HomeChata does not modify, recompute, or derive FEMA values; figures and classifications shown reflect FEMA's published data and are subject to revision in subsequent FEMA releases.

Flood Hazard. FEMA periodically revises flood maps and zone designations through formal map amendment processes, including Letters of Map Revision (LOMR). A property's flood zone classification may change between FEMA releases. HomeChata's data is refreshed periodically and may not reflect FEMA's most recent map updates. The flood zone designations shown are FEMA's official classifications, presented for informational purposes only. Flood zone designations describe modeled risk and do not guarantee whether flooding will or will not occur at any specific property. Users should verify a property's current flood zone designation through FEMA's official Flood Map Service Center before relying on flood zone information for any insurance, financing, or risk-management decision.

Disaster History. Disaster declaration data reflects only events that have received an official federal declaration from the President or FEMA. Local emergencies, weather events, and other incidents that did not result in a federal declaration are not included. Disaster declaration records reflect historical events and are not predictive of future incidents. FEMA updates its disaster declaration records on its own schedule, and HomeChata's data is refreshed periodically, which may result in a lag between FEMA's most recent record and what is displayed within the App.

Natural Hazard Risk. HomeChata presents a curated selection of hazard risk ratings from FEMA's National Risk Index, prioritized for relevance to typical residential decisions. The data shown does not include every hazard category in FEMA's full NRI dataset. NRI ratings are modeled estimates derived from historical loss data, exposure analysis, and community vulnerability indicators. They represent FEMA's assessment of relative risk and are not predictions, guarantees, or forecasts of specific future events. For a complete National Risk Index profile for any location, users may consult FEMA's official NRI portal.

The FEMA data presented within HomeChata describes historical events, current classifications, and modeled risk assessments only. None of this data constitutes a forecast, prediction, or guarantee regarding the occurrence, severity, or impact of any future flood, disaster, or natural hazard event at any specific location.

Environmental and Amenity Data

HomeChata presents environmental and quality-of-life data, including air quality indicators and nearby points of interest, sourced from institutional monitoring programs and location intelligence providers. Environmental readings reflect area-level conditions and may not capture hyperlocal variations. Amenity information, including business listings, hours of operation, and distances, is approximate and subject to change. HomeChata is not responsible for the accuracy or currency of third-party listings.

Air quality readings reflect conditions at or near the time of the report and do not constitute a forecast or guarantee of future air quality at any location. Air quality can change rapidly due to weather, wildfires, and other transient factors.

HomeChata presents a limited number of nearby amenities in each category, selected on a best-effort basis from third-party location data. The amenities shown may not represent every nearby location in a given category, and a closer or more relevant option may exist but be absent from the source provider's index.

Data Estimates

In circumstances where granular data is unavailable for a specific searched location, HomeChata may present contextual estimates or regional approximations to provide a complete and informative experience. Such estimates are clearly informational in nature and may not precisely reflect conditions at the specific address searched.

Independent Verification

HomeChata is designed to be a powerful starting point for neighborhood research — not the final word. We strongly encourage users to supplement HomeChata insights with independent research through official government sources, local law enforcement agencies, school districts, municipal websites, and direct engagement with relevant institutions before making any significant housing, real estate, or relocation decisions.
