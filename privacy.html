1. No Access by Children Under 18

HomeChata is intended for users who are at least 18 years old. We do not knowingly solicit data online from or market online to children under the age of 18. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child under 18 has provided us with personal information, please contact us at contact@homechata.com and we will promptly delete that information.

2. What Information Do We Collect? How Do We Use It?

Information You Provide

When you create an account with HomeChata, we collect:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Password (encrypted and never stored in plain text)

If you sign up using Google or Apple, we receive your name and email from those services.

Information Collected Automatically

When you use HomeChata, we automatically collect:

  • Device information (device type, operating system version)
  • App usage data (searches performed, features used)
  • IP address
  • Crash reports and error logs

Addresses You Search

When you search for an address, we process that address to retrieve neighborhood data, including information about nearby amenities, air quality, crime statistics, sex offender proximity, and other publicly available information. We may store your recent searches locally on your device for convenience. If you save an address to your favorites, it is stored in our database associated with your account.

Credit Balance

We track the number of credits remaining in your account. This information is stored securely and synced across your devices. Credit purchases are processed through the Apple App Store or Google Play Store, and we do not receive or store your payment card information.

Location Data

If you use the "Use My Location" feature, we access your device's location to determine your current address. We do not continuously track your location. Location access is only used when you explicitly request it.

Crime Statistics and Public Data

HomeChata displays crime statistics and other publicly available data for addresses you search. Crime data is sourced from the FBI Crime Data Explorer (UCR Program) and is retrieved on-demand for each report. Sex offender proximity data is sourced from publicly available sex offender registries via licensed third-party providers. This data is not stored permanently. The data may include information about total crimes, violent crimes, property crimes, crime rates, historical trends, and registered sex offender counts within specified radius distances.

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide and maintain the HomeChata service
  • Process your credit purchases
  • Track your credit balance
  • Retrieve neighborhood data for addresses you search
  • Retrieve and display crime statistics, sex offender data, and other public information
  • Save your favorite addresses
  • Send important account notifications and service updates
  • Improve the app based on usage patterns
  • Respond to your support requests
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

Communications

If you create an account, you will receive transactional communications (including email) from us related to your account, purchases, and important service updates. These communications are necessary for the operation of the service and cannot be opted out of. We do not send promotional or marketing emails.

3. How Do We Protect Customer Information?

When you create an account or make a purchase, we use secure systems and industry-standard security measures to protect your information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Encryption of passwords (hashed and never stored in plain text)
  • Secure authentication tokens
  • Access controls and monitoring

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. If you have questions about security, please contact us at contact@homechata.com.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

HomeChata may use cookies and similar technologies to provide customized and convenient features. Cookies are small pieces of information stored by your browser or mobile app on your device. Our cookies do not contain personally identifying information.

We may use third-party analytics services to collect and accumulate data that helps us understand and analyze our users' experience. For example, we may collect data relating to device types, operating systems, app version, screen resolutions, and usage patterns. We may also track the number of visits, time spent in the app, features used, and other app usage information. This information is stored locally on your device or in aggregate form and helps us improve the app.

We do not track what individual users read or search beyond what is necessary to provide the service.

5. Will We Disclose Information We Collect to Outside Parties?

We do not sell, trade, rent, or otherwise transfer your personal information to others, except as outlined in this Privacy Policy. We reserve the right to disclose your information:

  • As required by law: When we believe disclosure is necessary to comply with a judicial proceeding, court order, subpoena, or legal process
  • To protect our rights: To enforce our Terms of Service or other agreements, or to protect the property, safety, and rights of Mainali Software Enterprises LLC, our users, or the public
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, sale of assets, or bankruptcy, your information may be transferred to the acquiring entity
  • With your consent: When you consent to sharing your information with third parties

Third-Party Services

HomeChata uses third-party services to operate the app, including:

  • FBI Crime Data Explorer — crime statistics (no personal user data transmitted)
  • Sex offender registry data providers — proximity data (geographic coordinates only)
  • Google Places API — nearby amenity and location data
  • Air quality monitoring services — environmental data
  • Apple Sign In and Google Sign In — authentication
  • Supabase — database and infrastructure services
  • RevenueCat — purchase management (purchases processed through Apple or Google; we do not receive or store your payment card information)
  • OpenAI — crime news summaries (city/state only, no personal data transmitted)

These third-party services may collect limited information as needed to provide their functionality. Your use of HomeChata is subject to the privacy policies of these third-party services.

Aggregate Information

We may share non-personal, non-individual statistical or demographic information in aggregate form with our partners or service providers for research, analytics, or other purposes. This aggregate information does not identify individual users.

Links to Other Websites

HomeChata may contain links to other websites or services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third-party websites. We recommend reviewing the privacy policies of any third-party websites you visit.

6. How Do We Allow Customers to Update or Change Their Personal Information?

You may update or change your personal information by:

  • Editing your profile within the HomeChata app
  • Contacting us at contact@homechata.com

To request removal from our email list or to delete your account, please contact us at contact@homechata.com.

Account Deletion

You may delete your account at any time. Account deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Upon account deletion:

  • Your personal information will be deleted within 30 days, except where required for legal purposes
  • Unused credits are forfeited and cannot be recovered or refunded
  • Your purchase history may be retained as required by law

7. Data Retention

We retain your account information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you services. If you delete your account, we will delete your personal information within 30 days, except where we are required to retain it for legal, tax, or regulatory purposes.

Credit purchase records are retained as required by law. Unused credits are forfeited upon account deletion.
